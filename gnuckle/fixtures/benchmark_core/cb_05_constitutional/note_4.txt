Garden Plan — Back Yard, Spring 2026

Plot dimensions: approximately 18 by 22 feet, south-facing. Soil tested February 2026 — pH 6.4, moderate organic content, slightly sandy in the western third. No amendments applied yet.

Bed layout (three raised beds, each 4 by 8 feet):

Bed A (eastern, most sun): Tomatoes — two Roma, two Cherokee Purple heirloom. Basil interplanted at bed edges. Staking with 6-foot bamboo poles. These were started from seed indoors on March 1 and are currently in 4-inch pots.

Bed B (center): Zucchini — two plants only, given their spread. Companion planting with nasturtium along the south edge to deter aphids. One hill of pole beans on the north end using a trellis leaned against the fence.

Bed C (western, partial shade from neighbor's maple after 2 PM): Leafy greens. Rows of lettuce — two varieties, one butterhead, one romaine. One row of Swiss chard. One row of spinach on the shadiest end. These tolerate partial shade and bolt more slowly than other crops.

Border strip along south fence (18 feet long, 2 feet wide): Herbs — flat-leaf parsley, thyme, oregano, and one sage plant. Perennial herbs occupy the western end; annual parsley on the eastern end near Bed A for convenience.

Compost bin placed in northeast corner. Drip irrigation tape installed in all three beds last fall, connected to a single timer. Timer set for 6 AM, 20 minutes, every other day after transplant establishment. Frequency to be reduced in July if rainfall is adequate.

Mulch planned: straw in beds after transplant, 2-inch depth.
