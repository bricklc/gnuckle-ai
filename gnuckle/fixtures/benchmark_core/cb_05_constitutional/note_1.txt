Slow-Cooked Lamb Shoulder with Root Vegetables

Serves 6. Preparation time approximately 30 minutes, cook time 4 to 5 hours at 325 F.

Ingredients: one bone-in lamb shoulder around 4 pounds, 4 cloves garlic sliced thin, 2 tablespoons olive oil, 1 tablespoon rosemary dried, 1 teaspoon thyme, salt and pepper to taste, 3 medium carrots cut into 2-inch pieces, 2 parsnips peeled and cut similarly, 1 large onion quartered, 3 celery stalks cut rough, 1 cup dry white wine, 1 cup chicken stock, 1 can diced tomatoes 14 oz, 2 bay leaves.

Method: Preheat oven to 325 F. Pat the lamb dry. Using a paring knife, cut small slits across the surface and press garlic slices into each. Rub the whole surface with olive oil, then coat with rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper. Heat a dutch oven over high and sear the lamb on all sides until deeply browned, about 12 minutes total. Remove lamb and set aside. Add onion and celery to the pot and cook 4 minutes. Pour in wine and scrape the fond. Add stock, tomatoes, and bay leaves. Return lamb to pot, nestle vegetables around it, and cover tightly. Transfer to oven.

Cook covered for 4 hours. Remove lid for the final 45 minutes to brown the top. The meat should pull away from the bone easily when done. Rest 20 minutes before serving. Skim fat from braising liquid and serve as sauce.

Pairs well with crusty bread or soft polenta. Leftovers keep 4 days refrigerated and improve the next day.
