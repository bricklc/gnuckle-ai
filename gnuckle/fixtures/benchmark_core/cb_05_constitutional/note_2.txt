Travel Log — Krakow, Poland — October 2025

Day 1. Arrived by train from Warsaw at 14:20. The station is centrally located and walking distance to the Old Town. Checked into a small guesthouse on Ul. Florianska, second floor, overlooking the street. Room was modest but clean. Walked the main market square in the late afternoon. The Cloth Hall was crowded. Ate dinner at a milk bar near the university district — beet soup, potato dumplings, pickled cucumber salad. Total cost under 40 PLN.

Day 2. Wawel Castle opened at 9:00. Joined a timed entry for the State Rooms and the Royal Tombs in the Cathedral. The Cathedral crypt holds Polish kings and national heroes including Kosciuszko and pilgrims leave flowers regularly. Cathedral tower climb was steep, single file. View over the Vistula was clear. In the afternoon walked down to the Kazimierz district. The old Jewish quarter has a dense concentration of synagogues — seven within a few blocks. The Remuh Synagogue is still active. The cemetery behind it dates to the 16th century. Stones stacked along the walls are fragments recovered after wartime destruction.

Day 3. Auschwitz-Birkenau site visit. Bus from the main bus station, journey about 90 minutes each way. The visit took approximately 4 hours including both the main Auschwitz I site and the Birkenau camp. The scale of Birkenau is difficult to process until you are standing at the end of the main rail line. Museum is free to enter but timed entry is required and should be booked in advance online.

Day 4. Rest day. Walked along the Planty park that rings the Old Town. Visited the Czartoryski Museum collection. Leonardo da Vinci's Lady with an Ermine was on display, properly lit in a small dedicated room.
