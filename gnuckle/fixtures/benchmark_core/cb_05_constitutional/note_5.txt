Deck Repair Checklist — Spring 2026

Structure inspection completed March 28. The deck is approximately 12 years old, pressure-treated lumber, attached to the house on the north side. The following items were identified.

Completed items:
- Replaced 3 decking boards showing significant rot near the house wall. New boards cut to match, secured with hidden fasteners.
- Tightened all visible lag screws at the ledger board connection. Two were noticeably loose.
- Replaced one joist hanger on the east end that had lost two of its four nails over time.
- Sanded and re-drove 11 surface nails that had popped above the deck surface.

Remaining items:
- Sand entire deck surface. Current finish is weathered and flaking in multiple areas. Use 60-grit belt sander for field, 80-grit orbital for edges and around posts.
- Apply two coats of semi-transparent deck stain, color Driftwood Gray, same product used in 2020. Allow 24 hours between coats. Do not apply if temperature below 50 F or rain expected within 24 hours.
- Replace two stair treads. Bottom tread has surface checking; second tread has end splits. Material already purchased and stored in garage.
- Re-secure the post base on the southwest corner post. The anchor plate has one missing bolt. Hardware ordered, arriving April 12.
- Inspect and re-caulk all penetrations where deck meets house siding. Current caulk is cracked in three locations.

Materials on hand: stain (2 gallons), sandpaper, new stair tread lumber. Missing: post base bolt (on order), exterior caulk (to purchase).
